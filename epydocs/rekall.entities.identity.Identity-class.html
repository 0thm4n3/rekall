__hash__(self)
(Hashing function)

source code 

Hashing an identity has a big caveat.

Because an identity is hashed on all of its indices, and comparisons between identities are done by intersection of indices, it is possible for two identities to have different hashes and still be equal if compared. This is fine in certain cases, such as with identities retrieved from the EntityManager (because they're already merged and therefore unique) but may result in unexpected behavior with identities made up outside of the normal collection mechanism.
